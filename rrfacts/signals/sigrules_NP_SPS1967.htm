NP and SP&S Signals

1967

RuleAspectNameIndication
240-A1StopStop before any part of train or engine passes the signal
240-A2Stop and ProceedStop before any part of train or engine passes the signal, then proceed at Restricted Speed through entire block
240-A3Flashing Stop and ProceedStop. Block occupied. Proceed at Restricted Speed
240-BPermissiveProceed at Restricted Speed without stopping. (Used with signal 240-A2)
240-CApproachProceed prepared to stop before any part of train or engine passes the signal
240-DClearProceed
240-ELimited ApproachProceed not exceeding 50 mph prepared to stop at next signal
240-F1Limited Clear Approach SlowProceed not exceeding 50 mph approach next signal not exceeding 15 mph
240-F2Limited Clear Approach MediumProceed not exceeding 50 mph approach next signal not exceeding 30 mph
240-GApproach LimitedProceed. Approach next signal not exceeding 50 mph
240-HLimited ClearProceed; 50 mph within interlocking limits Approach next signal not exceeding 50 mph
240-IMedium ApproachProceed not exceeding 30 mph prepared to stop at next signal
240-J1Medium Clear Approach SlowProceed 30 mph within interlocking limits. Approach next signal not exceeding 15 mph
240-J2Medium Clear Approach MediumProceed 30 mph within interlocking limits. Approach next signal not exceeding 30 mph
240-KApproach MediumProceed. Approach next signal not exceeding 30 mph
240-LMedium ClearProceed; 30 mph within interlocking limits
240-MSlow ApproachProceed not exceeding 15 mph prepared to stop at next signal
240-NSlow Clear Approach MediumProceed; 15 mph within interlocking limits. Approach next signal not exceeding 30 mph
240-OApproach SlowProceed. Approach next signal not exceeding 15 mph
240-P1Slow ClearProceed; 15 mph within interlocking limits
240-P2 See note belowSlow ClearProceed not exceeding 15 mph
240-RRestrictingProceed at Restricted Speed not exceeding 15 mph

Note: On most railroads the presence or absence of a number plate is significant only to distinguish between a Stop signal and a Stop & Proceed signal. However, on the NP and the SP&S the number plate is also significant on the Slow Clear signal.
